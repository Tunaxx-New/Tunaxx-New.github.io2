Coal

According to its chemical composition, coal is a mixture of high-molecular polycyclic aromatic compounds
with a high mass fraction of carbon, as well as water and volatile substances with small amounts of mineral
impurities that form ash when burning coal. Fossil coals differ from each other in the ratio of their constituent
components, which determines their heat of combustion.

Charcoal

The finished coal consists mainly of carbon. The advantage of using
charcoal instead of conventional wood burning is the absence of water
and other components. This allows charcoal to burn at higher temperatures
and emit very little smoke.
